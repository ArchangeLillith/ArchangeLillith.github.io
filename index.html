About Me

As my father was an engineer, I've been interested in the logic of things since I was small. Because of his logical mind and chouce of career, we always had computers in the house so from a young age I was playing around with software. This transcended into a love for video games through my high school years, and led to me taking a couple courses in college on visual basic. From there, I stepped into a career in healthcare, always intending on coming back to coding. This happened, finally, in 2022 when I enrolled in an MIT course to study web development. Before the class started I spent three months scanning the web, learning anything I could to give me a good basis for the course - HTML, CSS and some basic JavaScript. These came in handy, and I have mantained my 100% in the course thanks to my dedication and interest pushing me to go further and seek out individual projects to complete.
